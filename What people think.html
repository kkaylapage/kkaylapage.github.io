What people think:

We asked some student-athletes about their experiences in the sport!


When did you start swimming?


  • "Around the ages of 3-4"
  • "2022"
  • "12 years old"
  • "When I was 2 months old"
  • "About 5 years ago"

How do you feel after training? (eg. energised, happy, sleepy, hungry; ect.)


  • "Energised, happy, puffed"
  • "Dead"
  • "Hungry, tired, sore but happy"
  • "Tired, hungry, satisfied"

What is the best part about swimming?


  • "It's calming and keeps you fit, it's also easy on the body"
  • "Beating your own PB's"
  • "Gives me time to myself and to think. Or if I don't want to think it allows my brain to just shut off"
  • "Getting out of the water" 😂
  • "Pushing yourself"

What is the worst part about swimming?


  • "Training in the cold winter!"
  • "Chlorine 😅"
  • "The dedication required"
  • "Bad performances"
  • "The hours you have to put into it"
  • "Endurance sets"

Have you noticed any health benefits from swimming?


  • "Mentally and physically fitter"
  • "It's great for cardio and muscle development"
  • "Stronger lungs and breathing better"
  • "Increased metabolism and muscular endure and strength"
  • "Always feel good after, helps with rehab and mobility"

Summary

After looking at the results provided by people. It is evident that swimming presents as a positive experience for many. As well as the many health benefits it provides, people who take part in the sport report that they feel happier and more energised after training sessions. Some individuals even said that swimming acts as a form of “therapy”, helping them overcome struggles.
